The serious part

The humor comes strictly from deflation, never invention — the skill only mocks what is literally on the page, and never adds facts, motives, or events the post doesn't state.

The framing owes everything to Harry Frankfurt's On Bullshit: these posts usually aren't lies — they're indifferent to truth, with every word optimized for engagement instead of accuracy. That's what makes them deflatable: there's always a smaller, truer sentence underneath.

Each tell is grounded in real research. The sources, so you can read the actual documents:

  • Frankfurt — On Bullshit (2005) — the frame: bullshit as indifference to truth
  • Cialdini — Influence: The Psychology of Persuasion (1984) — the authority principle
  • Pennycook et al. — "On the reception and detection of pseudo-profound bullshit" (2015)
  • Green & Brock — "The role of transportation in the persuasiveness of public narratives" (2000)
  • McGlone & Tofighbakhsh — "Rhyme as reason in aphorisms" (2000)
  • Tversky & Kahneman — "Judgment under uncertainty" (1974)
  • Meta — "Fighting Engagement Bait on Facebook" (2017)

The full catalog, with the mechanism and a practical "how to spot it" test for each tell, ships inside the plugin: references/catalog.md. Ask the skill why a tell works and it answers from there — sources cited, roast off.
